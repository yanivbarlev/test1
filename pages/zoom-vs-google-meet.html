Zoom vs. Google Meet: The Ultimate Video Conferencing Showdown

Which platform is better for your team meetings, webinars, and virtual calls? We compare features, pricing, and ease of use.

By Upingi Team / Updated on September 29, 2024

Introduction: Virtual Meeting Leaders

Zoom and Google Meet are giants in video conferencing. Understand their different approaches and strengths before choosing.

Core Features Compared

From participant limits and meeting duration to recording options and integrations, we pit Zoom against Google Meet.

Feature Zoom Google Meet
Free Plan Participant Limit 100 (40-min limit) 100 (60-min limit)
Recording Local (Free), Cloud (Paid) Included (Workspace plans)
Breakout Rooms Yes (Free & Paid) Yes (Paid Workspace plans)
Integration Extensive Marketplace Strong with Google Workspace

Which Platform Suits Your Needs?

Zoom is great for: Large webinars, feature-rich requirements, non-Google environments, extensive app integrations.

Google Meet is great for: Teams heavily invested in Google Workspace, simplicity, browser-based access, cost-effectiveness (if already using Workspace).

Verdict: Declaring the Winner (For You)

Summarizing the pros and cons. Recommendation based on specific needs like webinar features, budget, and ecosystem integration.

Both offer reliable video conferencing, but the best choice depends on your context.

Recommendation: Go with Zoom for feature depth and broad compatibility. Choose Google Meet for seamless Google Workspace integration and simplicity.

Key Differences

  • Free Tier Limits: Zoom (40min) vs. Meet (60min)
  • Advanced Features: Zoom (More extensive, often earlier)
  • Primary Access: Zoom (App focus) vs. Meet (Web focus)
  • Ecosystem: Zoom (Standalone) vs. Meet (Google Workspace)
  • Webinar Capabilities: Zoom (Generally more robust)
