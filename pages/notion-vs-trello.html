Notion vs. Trello: Flexible Workspace or Simple Kanban?

We compare the versatile, block-based workspace Notion with the straightforward, visual Kanban board Trello to help you choose the right tool for your workflow.

By Upingi Team / Updated on November 19, 2025

Introduction: Defining the Contenders

Notion is an all-in-one workspace offering notes, databases, wikis, calendars, and project management tools, all built around a flexible block system. Trello is a simple, visual project management tool based on Kanban boards, cards, and lists, renowned for its ease of use.

While both can be used for task management, their core philosophies and feature sets differ significantly. Notion offers depth and customizability, while Trello prioritizes simplicity and visual workflow tracking.
